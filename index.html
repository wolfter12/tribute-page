Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Here's a time line of Ruth Bader Ginsburg life:

  • March 15, 1933 - Born in Brooklyn, New York City
  • June 23, 1954 - Graduates from Cornell with a bachelor of arts degree in government
  • 1955 - Gives birth to a daughter
  • 1956 - Enters the Harvard Law School, where she is one of only 9 women in a class of about 500 men.
  • 1959 - Earns her law degree at Columbia
  • 1970 - Becomes a Co-founders the Women's Rights Law Reporter, the first law journal in the U.S. to focus exclusively on women's rights.
  • 1972 - Ginsburgshe taught at Columbia Law School, where she became the first tenured woman and co-authored the first law school casebook on sex discrimination.
  • April 14, 1980 - Nominated by President Carter to a seat on the DC circuit appeals court which was vacated by Judge Harold Leventhal upon his death.
  • June 22, 1993 - Bill Clinton nominates Ginsburg as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court to fill the seat vacated by retiring Justice Byron White.
  • 2002 - Ginsburg was inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame.
  • September 28, 2005 - speech at Wake Forest University, Ginsburg said Roberts's refusal to answer questions during his Senate confirmation hearings on some cases was "unquestionably right".
  • 2009 - Ginsburg was named one of 100 Most Powerful Women
  • February 5-13, 2009 - Has surgery and treatment for early stages of pancreatic cancer.
  • March 17, 2009 - It is announced that Ginsburg will be undergoing chemotherapy to treat her pancreatic cancer.
  • August 9, 2010 - Receives the American Bar Association's highest honor, the ABA medal.
  • 2012 - Ginsburg was named one of Glamour magazine's Women of the Year
  • August 31, 2013 - Becomes the first Supreme Court justice to officiate at a same-sex marriage ceremony.
  • November 26, 2014 - Undergoes a heart procedure to have a stent placed in her right coronary artery.
  • 2015 - Ginsburg was named one of Time magazine's 100 most influential people
  • July 17, 2020 - Ginsburg announces that her cancer has reemerged and she has been undergoing chemotherapy since May. She says she is "fully able" to remain a member of the Court.
  • September 18, 2020 - Dies at the age of 87.
