Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Here's a time line of Ruth Bader Ginsburg life:

  • 1933 - Born in Brooklyn, New York City
  • 1954 - Graduates from Cornell with a bachelor of arts degree in government
  • 1955 - Gives birth to a daughter
  • 1959 - Earns her law degree at Columbia
  • 1970 - Co-foundes the Women's Rights Law Reporter, the first law journal in the U.S. to focus exclusively on women's rights.
  • 1972 - Teachs at Columbia Law School, where she became the first tenured woman and co-authored the first law school casebook on sex discrimination.
  • 1980 - Nominates by President Carter to a seat on the DC circuit appeals court which was vacated by Judge Harold Leventhal upon his death.
  • 1993 - Bill Clinton nominates Ginsburg as an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court on June 22, 1993, to fill the seat vacated by retiring Justice Byron White.
  • 2005 - speech at Wake Forest University, Ginsburg said Roberts's refusal to answer questions during his Senate confirmation hearings on some cases was "unquestionably right"
  • 2020 - Dies at the age of 87.
